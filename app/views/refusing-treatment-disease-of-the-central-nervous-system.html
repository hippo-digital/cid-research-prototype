Refusing treatment if I have a disease of the central nervous system

Diseases of the central nervous system cause damage to the cells of the brain or spinal cord. This can have a large impact on the way we think, move or react to things. Examples of these types of conditions include motor neurone disease (MND), Parkinson’s disease, multiple sclerosis (MS) and Huntington’s disease.

About diseases of the central nervous system

[Extra detail to add]

In the advanced stages, many diseases of the central nervous system can cause you to lose capacity to make decisions about your care and treatment. In this situation you might want to refuse treatments aimed at sustaining your life.

Do you want to include diseases of the central nervous system in your Advance Decision?

Some people with a disease of the central nervous system might lack capacity to make decisions about their treatment but still have what you consider an acceptable quality of life. Because of this, it’s important to think about when you would like your Advance Decision to take effect. You can choose whether you want your Advance Decision to take effect as soon as you lose capacity to make a decision about your treatment, or only when, after losing capacity, you also show certain symptoms or behaviours.
