Refusing treatment in other scenarios

If there are other scenarios in which you would like to refuse treatment, or if there are other treatments that you would like to refuse, you can write them here. Remember that what you say here will only come into effect if you don’t have capacity to make a decision for yourself.

When writing in this space you must write the treatments that you want to refuse AND the scenarios in which you would like to refuse them.

About writing your own refusal

If you would like to refuse treatment in a situation that isn’t covered by the scenarios in this form, you can write it here. For example, some people want to refuse CPR in every situation.

The scenarios you have seen so far all contain a refusal of ‘life-sustaining treatment’ but you might want to refuse a treatment that isn’t life-sustaining. You might want to do this, for example, if you are allergic to certain medications. Also, you might not want to refuse all life-sustaining treatment and instead just refuse specific ones.

You must write the treatments that you want to refuse and the situations in which you want to refuse them. To be legally binding, it is not enough to write a general wish to not be treated.

If you feel that you would like to write something in this box but are unsure how to word it you can contact us by calling our free information line on 0800 999 2434 (open Monday to Friday, 11am – 3pm).
