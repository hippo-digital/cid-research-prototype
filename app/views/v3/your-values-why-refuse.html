Your values

Help doctors understand why you want to refuse treatment

If you lose mental capacity, it's important for the doctor treating you to know why you've made your advance decision.

! Warning

By explaining the reasons, your doctor can be confident that you understand and have considered the decisions you've made.
Read more about life-sustaining treatment

Some people have included statements like:

  • I have carefully considered the refusals of treatment included within this form.
  • I’m making this Advance Decision because I wish to avoid a situation where my life is prolonged even though my quality of life is severely diminished and I am unable to make my own decisions and participate in conversations. To me quality of life means...
  • I fear the prospect of having my life prolonged with either a loss of decision making capacity or a loss of physical ability that would lead me to depend on others far more than I fear death.
  • I review this decision regularly and do not need another opportunity to reconsider my decision or change my mind.
  • I could not tolerate being unable to interact meaningfully with my family and friends. By ‘meaningfully’ I mean being able to make and enjoy a joke, express my opinions, and provide a listening ear to those who need it.
  • Quality of life is much more important to me than quantity. This advance decision is an application of the principles that I hold dear and that I have held for a long time.

For example, some people make an advance decision because they have seen someone close to them being given a treatment that they would not want themselves, or there may be a particular situation that they want to avoid.
