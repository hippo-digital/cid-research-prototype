Signing and witnessing

You must sign your advance decision in the presence of a witness, who must also sign the form.

Your advance decision is not legally binding until it has been signed and witnessed.

What if I cannot sign the form?

If you are unable to sign the form, you can ask someone else to sign it on your behalf in your presence. If you do this:

  • the person signing should write a statement next to the signature explaining that you have directed them to sign on your behalf
  • your witness cannot be the same person who signed the form on your behalf
! Warning

Advance decisions are legally binding in England, Wales, and Northern Ireland. They are not legally binding in Scotland but it should still be taken into account by doctors.
Who can be my witness?

Your witness can be anyone over the age of 18. It can be a friend or relative.

If possible, you should not ask a close relative, partner, anyone who will inherit your money or property after your death, or your lasting power of attorney to be your witness. This is to avoid someone later questioning if you were put under pressure to make your advance decision.

Sharing your form

Once you have completed your advance decision you will need to share it widely so that it is known about and can be followed.

Can I share a photocopy?

Yes, the law says a photocopy is valid and can be used to inform any decision made about your care and treatment.

You should share a copy with your:

  • relatives
  • close friends
  • GP surgery

Different parts of the NHS do not always share medical records electronically. So it can be a good idea to also share a copy with your:

  • local hospital
  • local ambulance trust

You can use the NHS website to find your local hospital. You need to ask the patient records team at your local ambulance trust how your advance decision can be recorded.

What happens in an emergency?

An advance decision should be followed but in an emergency paramedics may not know about it. Paramedics might look in your bag for details of who you are. To help them find your advance decision you can carry a copy in a clearly marked envelope in your bag.

When you’re at home you can keep a copy in an obvious place. You can use the Lions Club Message in a Bottle scheme to keep a copy of your advance decision in the fridge. Paramedics should know to look for the Lions Club symbol when entering your house and to check the fridge.

If you’ve already completed your advance decision, you can order a wallet-sized notice card to keep with you in case of an emergency.
